NICE RECOMMENDS FINTEPLA▼ (FENFLURAMINE) FOR TREATING SEIZURES ASSOCIATED WITH DRAVET SYNDROME

Dear ,

We are delighted to confirm that the National Institute for Care and Health Excellence (NICE) has completed its review of Fintepla (fenfluramine) in the treatment of seizures associated with Dravet syndrome.

In the final appraisal document (FAD), NICE recommends fenfluramine as an add-on to other antiepileptic medicines for treating seizures associated with Dravet syndrome in people aged 2 years and older, only if 1:

  • it is an add-on to 2 other antiepileptic medicines (AEMs)
  • the frequency of convulsive seizures is checked every 6 months, and fenfluramine is stopped if it has not fallen by at least 30% compared with the 6 months before starting treatment
  • the company provides fenfluramine according to the commercial arrangement.
